Specialized therapeutic foster parents provide care to teens with challenging behaviors and work collaboratively with SailFuture’s mental health counselors, case managers, and support team.

Who do we serve?

Level I specialized therapeutic foster care is for recipients with a history of abuse or neglect, or delinquent behavior, and who have an emotional disturbance or serious emotional disturbance.

Male and female teens between 13 - 17 years old

What it Takes to Become A
Foster Parent

A better way to send money.

Home Life
Your home life should align with the stress and time commitment necessary to meet the complex needs of a child with significant trauma.

Prior understanding of complex behaviors, a strong support system, and flexible employment are critical to a successful home.
Home Study
A home study is a comprehensive assessment of your ability to care for a child. The home study can take up to 4 weeks to complete.

This includes a review of your physical home, your income, criminal and legal background, and medical history, among other elements.
Specialized Training
Specialized Therapeutic Foster Parents participate in 24 hours of parenting training.

Complete 33 hours of specialized therapeutic care training.
