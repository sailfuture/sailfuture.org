Instructional Approach

A SailFuture Academy graduate is equipped with the academic, vocational, and emotional abilities to transition into adulthood and successfully navigate the workforce, personal relationships, and household affairs.

Student Goals / Outcomes

A SailFuture Academy graduate is equipped with the academic, vocational, and emotional abilities to transition into adulthood and successfully navigate the workforce, personal relationships, and household affairs.

Students begin their journey at the SailFuture Academy in the ninth grade, a critical year to ensure vital, foundational knowledge is acquired. As students progress through grades 9-12, skills and concepts from the previous year are reinforced and expanded upon.

At the conclusion of a student’s time with SailFuture Academy, we guarantee placement in either a career or in a post-secondary institution. Additionally students leave with a comprehensive digital portfolio that details a curated body of work that represents their knowledge as well as provides a succinct inventory of acquired skills and certifications.

Goals for Graduates

At the conclusion of their time at SailFuture Academy, graduates possess the ability to think critically, speak effectively, and confidently approach problems with a clear understanding of their own strengths and areas of opportunity.

Specifically, a SailFuture Graduate will:

  • 100% of students will be placed in college or a living-wage career upon graduation.

  • Read and write at or above 11th grade level at the time of matriculation, as measured by NWEA MAP testing.

  • Proficient in Math at or above 11th grade level at the time of matriculation, as measured by NWEA MAP testing.

  • Have a minimum of 5 industry certifications, a completed Personality Index profile, and a comprehensive digital portfolio of work

  • Completion of 1,000 hours of vocational training

  • Completion of a paid, onsite apprenticeship in 11th grade

  • Completion of a paid, offsite work-study program in 12th grade

  • Design, craft and propose a comprehensive business plan including budgetary components, market research, financial viability, and detailed business model to a panel of the SailFuture business council and members of the St. Petersburg community.

Explanation of rationale for achievement standards for academic areas

Students are challenged to apply their knowledge acquired across disciplines to solving real-world problems while at the same time establishing a comprehensive understanding of oneself and how they seek to uniquely market their individual brand. Each academic area provides the foundation and the framework for students to seamlessly transition from introductory understanding to advanced application through a scaffolded four year curriculum design. Students demonstrate their achievement formatively as well as summatively through activities such as creating, presenting and defending multi-step, cross-disciplinary projects, proposals, and technical skill assessments.

Student 4-Year Journey

The 9th-grade year is critical to establish culture and trust, assess competency and develop individual learning needs, and to provide students with exposure and foundational skills for future learning in business, culinary, construction, and maritime coursework.

The data for our target student tells us that most of the students will join us well behind grade level in many subject areas. We anticipate significant differentiation for students within a classroom space, and, therefore, will activate support services and intervention specialists in reading, writing, mathematics, and English language.

ELA and STEM coursework is anchored into the theme of business ownership and all coursework is integrated into the blocks and all-day lab experiences in the SailFuture social enterprises.

Additionally, SailFuture Academy has identified targeted core values that our students will exhibit at school, at home, in the community, and in all their work, which, too, are interwoven across courses and terms.

Mechanisms for periodic assessment

Exceptional instruction combines a clear understanding of content and pedagogy. Put another way, excellent teachers have a clear understanding WHAT students should learn and HOW to best guide students toward those learning outcomes. Our approach to curriculum support is a hands-on approach in so much that it provides a clearly defined curriculum with measurable objectives and analysis resources, which helps set a clear vision for the WHAT and HOW of uniquely differentiated instruction.

As a school, we will provide a detailed curriculum with explicit lesson plans for each subject to ensure that the content across disciplines not only aligns with and exceeds state and national learning standards but also clearly demonstrates the interconnectivity of the subject areas. In the development of the curriculum, state and national education standards were utilized as a framework to determine the best-fit standard for each component of a thematic unit including but not limited to: Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS), Language Arts Florida Standards (LAFS) and Florida’s B.E.S.T Standards: English Language Arts, Florida Social Studies (FSS) and Next Generation Sunshine State Standards (NGSSS), and Mathematics Florida Standards (MAFS) and Florida’s B.E.S.T. Standards: Mathematics.

Our leaders and teachers will use term-end summative projects as a means of backward planning to define rigorous outcomes and objectives in order to provide a clear roadmap for how to get students to content and skill mastery. Each term will be centered around a theme that is universally addressed and integrated across all disciplines and learning components. During the planning week prior to a term, all grade-level faculty will work through the lesson plans in their respective subject area in order to ensure collaborative and meaningful across disciplines. Additionally, each thematic focus will outline the overarching target objectives for knowledge and skill acquisition as well as content-specific objectives to ensure appropriate mastery progress within a subject area.
The Academy’s leadership team will provide each core subject with the term-end summative project and accompanying rubric for each term as well as the lesson plans with clearly defined objectives, measurement indicators for those objectives, and opportunities for individualized student differentiation. In addition to the term-end summative assessments, students will have mini-project formative assessments that assess student acquisition of the designated weekly objectives and outcomes. Students will receive narrative feedback on each assessment, formative and summative, in order to transition student thinking from grade-driven motivation to growth-driven motivation. This also serves to minimize the comparison and social hierarchy that arises from perceptions of academic ability as prescribed by a letter or number grade.

Examples of formative assessment include but are not limited to: preparation of original discussion questions, interactive learning checks, short written or verbal responses with evidence, completion of practical application problem sets increasing in complexity, translation and computation of full-size model to scale model, and appropriate classification and measurement of variable to reach a desired outcome. Term-end summative assessments may include: a written project proposal accompanied by renderings, advertisement components, and budget spreadsheets that is then supported by a 4-6 minute presentation with slides immediately followed by a live question and answer session; creation of scale models of boats with viable electrical grids to support required boat functions with minimal dropped amperage; removal of carpet, replacement of subflooring with appropriate measurements and cuts using power tools, and installation of new vinyl laminate plank flooring with minimal waste due to incorrect measurement and mapping.

Methods for addressing poor student performance

The school will employ a holistic approach to student learning intervention. Nationally normed assessment tools will be utilized to benchmark student performance with the understanding that the data collected is a method of determining internal improvement to best serve the academic needs of each student.

Universal Screening

All students will be assessed upon entry to determine performance relative to grade level standards. We will do this using a number of different measures including the NWEA MAP Growth assessments for Language Arts, Reading, Math, and Science, STAR Reading, and STAR Math.

This data will serve as both intake, benchmarking, and exit data in order to inform teaching practices, curriculum progression, and areas needing additional targeted instruction. Students will not be grouped by performance level on the basis of maintaining diverse learning communities. NWEA assessment will take place 3 times a year: the beginning of term 1, the beginning of term 3, and the end of term 5. STAR assessments will complement the NWEA data by serving as ongoing accountability data between MAP testing dates for those students needing additional support.

Intervention

In an instance when intake and/or benchmarking data indicates that a 9th grade student is in need of focused instruction in a particular area or subject, as evidenced by an NWEA RIT below average and/or STAR Reading and Math Cut-scores are less than 812 and 787, respectively, a tailored learning plan will be implemented through the lens of the current unit. Faculty, administration, and support staff will collaboratively develop an individualized plan, shared with all parties that interact with the student, that appropriately targets the most significant area(s) of focus while maintaining commensurate academic rigor and objectives of the lesson and unit.

Each plan will include measurable goals that are realistic yet ambitious for an 8-week period, classroom modifications (if needed), computer curriculum focus (if needed), and a viable schedule to work with the student one-on-one. Regular updates regarding student progress academically as well as professionally will be maintained via professionalism scores and narrative feedback focused on the measurable goals outlined in the plan. When a student has reached an average and/or above average NWEA RIT score and/or STAR Reading and Math Cut-scores greater than 1049 and 899, respectively, the supplemental STAR assessments will gradually diminish.

School Calendar and Daily Schedule

SailFuture’s annual calendar is designed to meet the needs of our students, faculty, and our unique school model.

The year-round, 5-term academic calendar provides meaningful breaks to students and faculty throughout the year, as well as ample planning time for faculty to regroup and prepare high-quality and individualized lesson plans for their students.

Students attend class for 8-9 weeks each term which is followed by a 2- week break for students and a week of break and a week of planning for faculty. This academic calendar allows for a more evenly distributed allocation of time in the classroom and time away from school thereby reducing the impact of an extended summer vacation. Similarly, faculty has a week of protected, dedicated planning time prior to each term in order to assess and improve content delivery.
